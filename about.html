About CUNYStudy

We built this because we needed it. A study group finder made by CSI students, for CSI students.

The Problem We Solved

Why we built CUNYStudy instead of a generic class project.

94% of CSI students commute

Unlike residential campuses, students at the College of Staten Island come to class and go home. There are no dorms, no common rooms — and no easy way to find classmates who want to study together after class.

GroupMe is not a matchmaker

Students use Discord and GroupMe to chat with people they already know. But there was no tool to connect students in the same class who have matching schedules and want to form a group. That is the gap CUNYStudy fills.

Study groups improve grades

Research shows that students who participate in collaborative study groups score significantly higher on exams and assignments compared to students who study alone. We wanted to make that advantage accessible to every CUNY student.

No CUNY-specific tool existed

We searched. There was nothing like this for CUNY students. GroupMe is just a chat app. Blackboard only shows students in your own section. We built the tool we wished existed — for every course, every campus.

What We Built With

Every technology covered in CSC 226 — Web Database Applications.

HTML5

Semantic structure across all 5 pages. Forms, navigation, accessible markup, and meta viewport tags for every screen size.

CSS3

Custom design system with CSS variables, flexbox and grid layout, animations, hover effects, and full mobile responsiveness.

JavaScript

Dynamic rendering, localStorage database simulation, live search and filter, form validation, count-up animations, and DOM manipulation.

SQL

Full schema in database.sql — SELECT, INSERT, UPDATE, DELETE, LEFT JOIN with USING, LIKE, IN, BETWEEN, CASE WHEN, UNION, GROUP BY, and more.
